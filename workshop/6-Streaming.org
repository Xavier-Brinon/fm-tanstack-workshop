* Objectives

* The one tradeoff with ssr
We talked about the SPA tradeoff. It sucked when the user browsed to our
app and saw this

#+caption: SPA Flow
#+attr_html: :width 500px
[[file:./img/1_img1.png]]

This happened before our initial render only, essentially, rendered a
shell for our app with a loading spinner.

It rendered this quickly, for sure, but the requests for data did not
happen until that initial response was received, and our browser parsed,
and executed our javascript. Only then did our requests for data fire.

SSR is cool because instead of a loading spinner, our browser renders
full content from that initial response.

#+caption: SSR Flow
#+attr_html: :width 500px
[[file:./img/1_img3.png]]

** What are we assuming though
Until that initial render, this is what the user sees.

#+caption: Browser waiting
#+attr_html: :width 500px
[[file:./img/6_img1.png]]

SPAs always render fast, but show useless content (like a spinner) until
the data are finished loading.

An equivalent apps with SSR will always render the final product sooner
(since the data loading starts sooner), but the user may see /nothing/
for an uncomfortable amount of time.

Can we get the best of all worlds?

* Streaming
*START* loading the data on the server, but render the rest of the page
(possibly just a spinner) immediately.

When the data finish loading, the server /pushes/ it down.

Best of all worlds.

#+caption: Browser waiting
#+attr_html: :width 500px
[[file:./img/6_img2.png]]

This is your feature to use. Use it as much, or as little as you want.

But don't over use it.

Too many pieces of data plopping into existence can create a jarring
effect for the user.
